    Welcome to the Maritz Lab at UWC

    Our research seeks to understand the functional roles of snakes in African ecosystems. We have several ongoing projects that address questions regarding snake natural history, ecology, communities, and conservation. Our interests and questions span a number of study systems. We have active study sites at several nature reserves around the country including Koeberg, Benfontein, Tswalu, and Kruger. Several projects in the lab address the spatial and foraging ecology of snakes as well as the population dynamics of snakes and their prey. We utilize a number of approaches to collect the data required to answer these questions. Our approaches include intensive sampling in the field using funnel traps, cover boards, and radio-telemetry which are fed into mark-recapture analyses. Other projects are exclusively computational and seek to model the distribution of a species or to analyze citizen-collected data. While our interest extends to all snakes species, currently, our attention has been focused on Cape Cobra Naja nivea, Boomslang Dispholidus typus, Puff Adder Bitis arietans), whip snakes (Psammophis spp., Rhombic Egg-eater (Dasypeltis scabra), and Plain Mountain Adder (Bitis inornata).

    For more information, check out our research page.
