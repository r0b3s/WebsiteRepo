    Jean-Jacque Forgus

    MSc student

    My project aims to characterize feeding guilds and ecological functional groups within the tetrapod faunal community at Koeberg Nature Reserve. Specifically, I plan to examine whether the snake community in the reserve fits within these existing groups of species (potentially providing the ecosystem with functional redundancy), or whether their natural history makes snakes functionally unique. Although focused on snakes, my project relies heavily on citizen science approaches (e.g., SABAP2) and active camera trapping to gather information regarding faunal diversity for other tetrapod assemblages.

    Janine Greuel

    MSc student

    Sociable weavers (Philetairus socius) are important ecosystem engineers across their geographic distribution. However, weaver populations are known to be heavily top-down regulated through predation by Cape Cobras (Naja nivea) and Boomslang (Dispholidus typus). My project, in collaboration with the UCT Sociable Weaver Project, aims to quantify patterns of colony usage by these two snake species in order to better understand their role as predators in the system.

    Jody Taft

    MSc student

    My project aims to clarify taxonomic uncertainties around the Vulnerable Cape Whipsnake (Psammophis leightoni) and Least Concern Namib Whipsnake (Psammophis namibensis). Given concerns around the taxonomic validity of P. leightoni, and its implications for whether or not the species should be granted formal protection, I am using morphological, phylogenetic, and species distribution modelling approaches to test species boundaries within this group.
